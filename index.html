About Me

I'm naturally curious and a quick learner. I spend a lot of my free time exploring new technologies, experimenting with different approaches, and improving the way I build software. Learning isn't something I do only when a project requires it—it's part of how I stay sharp.

As an indie developer, I also think beyond writing code. I care about building products that are maintainable, reliable, and practical. Every technical decision has a trade-off, and I enjoy finding the balance between development speed, scalability, and long-term value.

How I Work

I believe technology is a tool, not the goal.

I don't choose frameworks because they're trending. I choose them because they're the right fit for the problem, the project, and the business behind it. The best solution isn't always the one using the newest technology—it's the one that's reliable, maintainable, and delivers value.

I'm always learning new tools, but I don't adopt them just because they're popular. New technology should earn its place by making the product better, not by making the tech stack look more impressive.

If you already have a preferred stack, I'm happy to work with it. If you don't, I'm comfortable making those decisions for you based on what makes the most sense.

And if your project requires a technology I haven't used before, that doesn't automatically mean it's a blocker. In many cases, I can build the solution you need with the tools I already know well, saving development time while keeping the focus on what really matters: solving your business problem.
